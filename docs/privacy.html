Privacy Policy

HMB Sailing Log – Android App

Last updated: 2026-07-22  |  App: HMB Sailing Log  |  Developer: LacoSte© / IO986  |  Contact: steclaco@gmail.com

1. Introduction

HMB Sailing Log ("the App") is a personal sailing logbook for Android. This Privacy Policy explains what data the App collects, how it is used, and your rights as a user.

2. Data Collected and Why

2.1 Location (GPS)

  • What: Device GPS coordinates (latitude, longitude, speed, course)
  • Why: To record your sailing route, create automatic logbook entries during tracking, and show your position on the map
  • Storage: Stored locally on your device only. Never sent to third-party servers without your explicit action.
  • When: Only while a Tracking session is actively running.

2.2 Online Account (Optional)

  • What: Email address and display name
  • Why: To synchronize your logbook to the HMB cloud service (logbook.hmba.boats)
  • Storage: Stored on the HMB cloud server. Your email is used only for authentication and is never sold or shared.
  • Optional: The App is fully functional without creating an account.

2.3 Skipper Profile & Vessel ID

  • What: Skipper name, sailing license, VHF/SRC license number, vessel call sign, MMSI
  • Why: To autofill the Mayday card and PDF voyage export
  • Storage: Stored locally using Android SharedPreferences. Never transmitted automatically.

2.4 Logbook Entries, Photos, Tracks

  • What: Text notes, photos attached to entries, GPS track points
  • Why: To form your personal sailing logbook
  • Storage: Local SQLite database. Synced to HMB cloud only if you are logged in and explicitly sync.

2.5 App Preferences

  • What: Language setting, unit preferences, marine instrument connection settings
  • Storage: Stored locally using Android SharedPreferences.

2.6 Cloud Export to Google Drive (Optional)

  • What: A PDF summary and a GPX track file for each sailing day you close (route, log entries, weather, crew/duty notes you entered)
  • Why: So your logbook survives even if the phone is lost or damaged at sea — it uploads automatically once you finish editing the day, no extra tap needed
  • Sign-in: Uses Google Sign-In, requesting only the drive.file scope — the narrowest Drive scope that exists. It lets the App see and manage only files it creates itself; it cannot browse, read, or modify anything else already in your Drive
  • Storage: Uploaded to your own Google Drive, under a folder named HMB_Sailing_Log_DATA. We never receive or store a copy — the files live in your Drive, under your Google account, subject to Google's own privacy policy
  • Optional: Off by default. Without signing in, nothing is uploaded — everything stays local
  • Your control: Revoke access anytime from your Google Account's "Third-party apps with account access" settings, or delete the files/folder directly in Google Drive

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes. Your data may be shared only:

  • With the HMB cloud (logbook.hmba.boats) if you create an account and sync
  • With your own Google Drive, if you enable Cloud export and sign in (§2.6) — the App can only create/manage files it made itself there, never access anything else in your Drive
  • When you explicitly export a PDF or GPX file and share it via Android's share functionality

5. Data Retention

  • Local data: Remains on your device until you delete the App or clear its data
  • Cloud data: Deleted within 30 days of account deletion
  • Google Drive exports: Remain in your Google Drive until you delete them yourself — we have no way to delete files from your Drive remotely
  • Exported files: Remain in your device's storage until deleted manually

7. Children's Privacy

The App is not intended for users under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

8. Your Rights (GDPR – EU Users)

If you are located in the European Union, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access – Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to rectification – Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure – Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to portability – Receive your data in a machine-readable format (JSON/CSV via export)
  • Right to object – Object to processing of your personal data

To exercise any of these rights, contact: steclaco@gmail.com

9. Data Security

  • All local data is stored in Android's application sandbox, inaccessible to other apps
  • Cloud communication uses HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • Passwords are never stored in plain text
